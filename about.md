    <h2 style="margin-top: 0;">My Story</h2>
    <p>
    I am a second-year PhD student in Quantum Computing at the <strong>University of Strasbourg</strong>. I was born in Milan and raised in Cinisello Balsamo. In high school I attended classical studies at <strong>Liceo Classico Giulio Casiraghi</strong> in Cinisello Balsamo and I developed a taste for humanities, while at university I changed my path towards STEM and I graduated in Mathematics. My main interest at the time was Geometry in its more topological and differential counterparts. I can clearly state that my favourite math book at the time was <a href="https://pi.math.cornell.edu/~hatcher/AT/ATpage.html" target="_blank">Allen Hatcher's "Algebraic Topology"</a>. Nowadays, I have taken some distance from the pure hardcore mathematics, but I still suffer from its fascination. I studied at <strong>University of Milano-Bicocca</strong> for my Bachelor's and at <strong>Università Statale di Milano</strong> during my Master's. I also spent six months at the <strong>Mathematisches Institut der Universität Bonn</strong> as Erasmus student in 22/23. After graduation I worked as test engineer for almost a year and then I resolved myself to start a PhD. Presently, I am a second year PhD student in Quantum Physics at <strong>Centre Européen de Sciences Quantiques</strong> (<a href="https://www.cesq.eu" target="_blank">CESQ</a>) and <strong>Institut de science et d'ingénierie supramoléculaires</strong> (<a href="https://isis.unistra.fr" target="_blank">ISIS</a>). I am mostly concerned with aspects of quantum computing such as quantum optimal control and quantum error correction.
    </p>
